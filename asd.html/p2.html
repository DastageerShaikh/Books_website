Capital:- Pataliputra

Year Active:-500+ year

The Mauryan Empire was one of the largest and most powerful empires in the history of India. Founded by Chandragupta Maurya in 322 BCE, it expanded across the Indian subcontinent, covering modern-day India, Pakistan, and parts of Afghanistan and Bangladesh. The empire reached its peak under the reign of Ashoka the Great, who ruled from 268 to 232 BCE. Ashoka is famous for his conversion to Buddhism and efforts to spread its teachings across Asia. The Mauryan administration was highly organized, with a centralized government and a network of spies to maintain order. The economy flourished through agriculture, trade, and commerce. The Mauryan Empire also made significant advancements in art, architecture, and science. However, after Ashoka's death, the empire gradually weakened and eventually fell in 185 BCE. Despite its decline, the Mauryan Empire left a lasting legacy on Indian civilization.
